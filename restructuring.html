Service — 01

Restructuring

When a company reaches a point of financial stress, the window for effective action narrows quickly. We help owners, boards, and lenders navigate that window — stabilizing operations, managing stakeholders, and building a path to recovery before options are exhausted.

Overview

Stabilize. Separate.
Restore.

Restructuring is the disciplined process of separating what's viable from what isn't — eliminating the debt, cost, or operational drag that is suffocating an otherwise sound business. The goal is not simply to survive a crisis, but to emerge with a sustainable capital structure and a clear path to profitability.

We work with companies across the spectrum of distress: from early-stage liquidity stress requiring a lender negotiation, to complex multi-creditor situations requiring coordinated out-of-court restructurings or Chapter 11 proceedings. In every case, we move quickly, communicate directly with all stakeholders, and focus relentlessly on preserving and recovering value.

Our principals have represented debtors, creditors, and acquirers in restructuring situations — giving us a practical, multi-perspective view of how these processes resolve and what drives outcomes.

When to Engage
  • Loan covenant defaults or lender forbearance requests
  • Cash flow insufficient to service existing debt
  • Vendor or trade creditor pressure mounting
  • SBA loan default or demand letter received
  • Board or equity sponsor seeking independent assessment
  • Management team stretched beyond capacity
  • M&A transaction complicated by existing debt structure
Capabilities

What We Do

01

Financial Distress Assessment

An honest, rapid assessment of a company's financial position — cash runway, debt obligations, creditor priorities, and available options. We cut through complexity to give ownership and boards a clear, unvarnished picture of where things stand and what paths are available.

  • 13-week cash flow analysis
  • Debt and covenant review
  • Stakeholder mapping
  • Options analysis: in-court vs. out-of-court
02

Creditor Negotiations

Coordinated negotiation with secured lenders, unsecured creditors, and other stakeholders to achieve consensual restructuring outcomes. Lenders generally prefer recovery over litigation — we help both sides find the structure that maximizes value and closes efficiently.

  • Forbearance and standstill agreements
  • Debt modification and maturity extensions
  • Principal reduction and payoff negotiations
  • Multi-lender intercreditor coordination
03

Operational Turnaround

Financial restructuring alone does not fix a broken operating model. We work alongside management to identify and address the underlying operational drivers of distress — whether cost structure, margin deterioration, working capital inefficiency, or leadership gaps.

  • Operating cost reduction
  • Working capital optimization
  • Vendor and supply chain stabilization
  • Interim operational leadership
04

Chapter 11 Advisory

When an out-of-court resolution is not achievable, Chapter 11 can be an effective tool for restructuring debt, rejecting burdensome contracts, and conducting a going-concern sale. We advise debtors on the use of Chapter 11 as a strategic instrument — not a last resort.

  • Pre-filing strategy and planning
  • DIP financing coordination
  • Plan of reorganization development
  • 363 sale advisory
05

SBA Debt Workouts

SBA-guaranteed loans carry unique procedural requirements and government-side stakeholders that make workouts materially different from conventional bank debt. We have direct experience navigating the SBA's offer-in-compromise process, lender serviced workouts, and the interplay between personal guarantees and SBA recovery.

SBA distress requires early action — the SBA's collection apparatus, once engaged, is difficult to slow. The right representation, engaged before a loan is charged off, meaningfully expands the available options.

  • SBA 7(a) and 504 loan workouts
  • Offer in Compromise (OIC) preparation and negotiation
  • Deferral and modification requests
  • Personal guarantee exposure analysis
  • Lender serviced vs. SBA-held loan strategy
  • Coordination with SBA-approved lenders and CDCs
  • Treasury offset and collections avoidance
Our Process

How We Work

1

Rapid Assessment

We move quickly to understand the full picture — cash position, debt obligations, operational health, and stakeholder dynamics. Most initial assessments are completed within days, not weeks.

2

Stakeholder Communication

We establish direct, credible communication with all creditor constituencies. Silence and delay are the enemies of restructuring outcomes. Proactive, transparent communication builds the foundation for consensual resolution.

3

Structure the Resolution

We develop and negotiate the restructuring structure — whether a bilateral loan modification, multi-party out-of-court workout, or Chapter 11 plan — with a focus on speed, certainty, and maximum value recovery.

4

Execution & Close

We drive the process to completion. Restructurings stall when they lose momentum. We maintain pressure, manage the stakeholder dynamics, and keep the process on track through closing.

5

Post-Restructuring Stability

A successful restructuring creates a new starting point — not an ending point. We can remain engaged post-close to ensure the operational and financial improvements required to perform under the restructured obligations are achieved.
