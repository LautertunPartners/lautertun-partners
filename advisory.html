Advisory

At every significant inflection point — a sale, an acquisition, a succession, a strategic pivot — the quality of counsel matters more than the quality of the deal. We provide senior advisory to owners, boards, and leadership teams navigating these moments with independence, experience, and discretion.

Overview

Independent Counsel
at Critical Moments.

The lower middle market is full of capable operators who are experts in their industries. What those operators often lack at strategic inflection points is an independent, experienced voice — someone who has seen these transactions and transitions from multiple sides and can help navigate the complexity without a conflicting financial interest.

Our advisory work is built around a simple premise: our clients make better decisions when they have access to the same quality of strategic counsel that larger companies take for granted. We bring that counsel to the $5M–$50M market, where it is most scarce and most needed.

We do not take transaction fees based on deal size, which means our advice is not influenced by whether a deal closes or how large it is. Our incentive is always to get the right outcome for the client — not the largest fee for ourselves.

Situations We Address
  • Founder considering a sale or recapitalization
  • Business evaluating an acquisition or add-on
  • Private equity sponsor needing operating advisory
  • Board seeking independent assessment of management plan
  • Company at a strategic crossroads with no clear path forward
  • Leadership transition requiring structured planning
  • Lender or equity sponsor needing a second opinion
Capabilities

What We Do

01

M&A Advisory

We advise business owners and boards on sale processes, acquisitions, and recapitalizations — from initial positioning and buyer identification through negotiation, due diligence, and closing. Our focus is on process discipline and transaction certainty, not transaction size.

  • Sale process management
  • Buyer identification and outreach
  • Letter of intent and purchase agreement negotiation
  • Due diligence management
  • Recapitalization and minority investment advisory
02

Strategic Planning

Meaningful strategic planning is not an annual retreat exercise. It is a rigorous process of honestly assessing where a business stands, what the market requires, and what the organization is actually capable of. We facilitate and build strategic plans that are grounded in operational reality and financially defensible.

  • Competitive and market position assessment
  • Financial model development and stress testing
  • Growth strategy and capital allocation
  • Scenario and contingency planning
03

Transaction Support

Whether buying or selling, lower middle market transactions are operationally complex and move quickly. We provide the financial and strategic support that ensures our clients are not disadvantaged by the sophistication of the counterparty — regardless of which side of the table they sit on.

  • Quality of earnings review and interpretation
  • Management presentation preparation
  • Working capital and net debt negotiation
  • Post-closing integration planning
04

Board Advisory

Boards of lower middle market companies often lack the financial and operational expertise to effectively challenge management plans, evaluate strategic alternatives, or govern through periods of stress. We serve as independent board members or board observers, providing the rigor and accountability that effective governance requires.

  • Independent director services
  • Board committee support (audit, compensation, strategy)
  • Management plan review and challenge
  • Fiduciary process advisory in distressed situations
Our Approach

How We Advise

1

Understand the Situation Completely

Good advisory begins with honest diagnosis. We spend the time required to understand the business, the stakeholders, the history, and the objectives — before offering a point of view on strategy or process.

2

Frame the Alternatives Clearly

Every significant decision involves trade-offs. We map the realistic alternatives — including the ones clients are reluctant to consider — and pressure-test the assumptions behind each. Our job is to ensure clients make decisions with open eyes.

3

Provide a Direct Point of View

We are not consensus builders or information brokers. We form views, and we share them directly. Clients who want validation of a decision already made are not a good fit. Clients who want an experienced, independent perspective are.

4

Execute with Discipline

Strategic advice that does not translate into execution is academic. We drive the processes we advise on — managing timelines, counterparties, and the inevitable complications that arise in any significant transaction or transition.
