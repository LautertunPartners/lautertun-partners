Service — 03

Financial Leadership
& Management

Many lower middle market companies are managed by operators who excel at their craft — but lack the financial infrastructure and leadership that protects and grows what they've built. We embed experienced financial executives directly into your organization, delivering institutional-grade financial management at the scale and cost structure your business actually needs.

Overview

The CFO Function,
Built for Your Scale.

A full-time CFO with the experience to manage a complex capital structure, lead lender relationships, and support M&A activity costs $300,000–$500,000 per year — before benefits, bonus, and equity. Most lower middle market companies cannot justify that investment on a permanent basis, but they absolutely need that capability when they face a difficult lender negotiation, a financing transaction, or an operational inflection point.

Our financial leadership engagements are structured to deliver senior financial executive capability on a fractional or interim basis — embedded in your organization, working alongside your team, and aligned with your specific situation and timeline.

We are not a staffing firm placing a contractor. We are experienced CFOs and financial executives who take genuine accountability for the financial function and its outcomes.

When You Need Us
  • CFO departure or gap in senior financial leadership
  • Company preparing for a financing or sale process
  • Lender requiring upgraded financial reporting and controls
  • Business growing faster than the finance function can support
  • Owner-operator managing finances personally and stretched thin
  • Post-acquisition integration requiring financial leadership
  • Distressed situation requiring a Chief Restructuring Officer
Capabilities

What We Do

01

Interim CFO

A senior financial executive embedded in your organization, serving as CFO on a full-time or part-time basis depending on need. We own the financial function, lead the team, manage external relationships, and support ownership and the board — not as a consultant, but as a member of your leadership team.

  • Full financial function ownership and leadership
  • Board and ownership financial reporting
  • Banking and lender relationship management
  • Financial team development and management
  • Strategic financial planning and modeling
02

Chief Restructuring Officer (CRO)

In distressed situations, creditors and courts often require an independent financial executive with specific restructuring authority. We serve as CRO — providing the operational credibility, financial expertise, and stakeholder management required to lead a company through restructuring while maintaining day-to-day operations.

  • Independent financial authority in distressed situations
  • Creditor and court reporting
  • Cash management and liquidity preservation
  • Operational stabilization alongside financial restructuring
03

Financial Reporting & Controls

Reliable financial reporting is the foundation of every other financial function — lender relationships, strategic decisions, and transaction readiness all depend on it. We build and improve reporting infrastructure, implement controls, and produce the financial statements that banks, equity sponsors, and buyers require.

  • Monthly close process design and management
  • Management reporting package development
  • Internal controls assessment and remediation
  • Audit preparation and support
  • GAAP compliance and accounting policy
04

Cash Management

Cash is the oxygen of a business. In stressed or high-growth situations alike, understanding and managing cash with precision is non-negotiable. We implement cash management disciplines — forecasting, variance analysis, and working capital optimization — that give ownership and lenders visibility and control.

  • 13-week and rolling cash flow forecasting
  • Accounts receivable and payable management
  • Working capital improvement
  • Banking structure and treasury optimization
05

Lender Reporting & Compliance

Lender relationships are managed — or mismanaged — through the quality and consistency of financial communication. We build the reporting infrastructure that keeps lenders informed, covenant compliance current, and the relationship on solid footing — or, when needed, we lead the communication that stabilizes a deteriorating credit relationship.

  • Borrowing base certificate preparation
  • Covenant compliance reporting and cure plans
  • Lender presentation and communication management
  • Credit facility amendment support
How It Works

Engagement Structure

1

Situation Assessment

We start by understanding the current state of the financial function, the specific gaps or pressures driving the need, and the timeline and goals of the engagement. Most assessments are completed in the first week.

2

Engagement Design

We structure the engagement around the actual need — time commitment, scope of authority, reporting relationships, and timeline. We are not selling a predetermined engagement model; we design engagements around what the situation actually requires.

3

Rapid Integration

We integrate quickly. Within the first few weeks, we establish relationships with the finance team, external accountants, lenders, and key stakeholders — and begin delivering on the immediate priorities that drove the engagement.

4

Build Permanent Capability

Where the goal is to transition to a permanent hire, we actively build the infrastructure, documentation, and team capability that makes that transition successful. We do not create dependency — we create organizational strength.
