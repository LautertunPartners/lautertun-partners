The Mark

A Symbol of
Clarification

Concept Breakdown

The mark abstracts the lauter tun's false bottom — the slotted vessel floor that separates valuable wort from spent grain. In business terms, this is the act of restructuring: filtering what's working from what isn't, and letting clarity rise.

Concentric Rings

The vessel itself. An outer and inner ring echoing the circular lauter tun in cross-section — contained, structural, purposeful.

Graduated Filter Lines

Seven horizontal lines that grow sparser toward the top — the false bottom's slots. Dense at base (complexity, debt, pressure), opening upward (clarity, resolution, growth).

Rising Vertical with Arrowhead

The clarified wort rising through the vessel — and simultaneously, an upward arrow representing turnaround, the company's trajectory restored.

False Bottom Bar

A horizontal base line with small descending stubs references the lauter tun drain spigots — the mechanism of separation and extraction.

Brand Voice & Usage

Guidelines

✓ Do

Use Cormorant Garamond for all display, headlines, and brand moments. Pair with DM Sans for body and UI. Use the wordmark in all-caps with generous tracking (0.06–0.10em). Place the mark on dark navy or parchment backgrounds. Allow generous white space — restraint is strength.

✗ Don't

Don't use the mark at sizes below 20px without switching to the simplified 4-line version. Don't place the logo on busy photographic backgrounds without a dark overlay. Don't use gold text on white backgrounds at small sizes (legibility fails). Never stretch or rotate the mark. Don't use more than two weights of either typeface in a single layout.

✓ Voice: Tone

Speak as a trusted, experienced partner — not a salesperson. Use plain, declarative sentences. Acknowledge difficulty before offering hope. Demonstrate competence through specificity. Our clients are under pressure; every word should reduce anxiety, not amplify it.

✗ Voice: Avoid

Avoid jargon-first communication ("leveraging synergistic value creation"). Don't be vague about outcomes. Avoid institutional coldness — this firm works with small business owners, not Fortune 500 legal departments. Never use urgency as a fear tactic.
